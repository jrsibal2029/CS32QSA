TOURIST ATTRACTIONS

KAYANGAN LAKE

Often called the Cleanest Lake of Asia, Kayangan Lake, nestled in the heart of Coron, is often heralded as one of the most beautiful lakes in the Philippines. Surrounded by towering limestone cliffs and lush greenery, this freshwater lake boasts crystal-clear waters that shimmer in stunning shades of blue and turquoise. Accessible via a short hike, visitors are rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views from the summit before descending to the tranquil shores of the lake. Renowned for its vibrant marine life, Kayangan Lake is a hotspot for swimming and snorkeling, offering a serene escape and a glimpse into the rich biodiversity of the region. Its pristine beauty and tranquil atmosphere make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

TWIN LAGOON

Twin Lagoon is one of Coron’s most breathtaking natural attractions, characterized by its stunning juxtaposition of freshwater and saltwater. This enchanting lagoon features two interconnected pools, enveloped by towering limestone cliffs and lush vegetation. Visitors can experience the exhilarating transition between warm and cool waters, creating a unique aquatic adventure. Accessible by a short swim through a small opening, the lagoons offer a peaceful respite and a perfect setting for snorkeling amidst colorful marine life. The dramatic scenery and serene ambiance make Twin Lagoon a must-visit destination for those exploring the beauty of Coron.

SIETE PECADOS

Siete Pecados is a captivating group of tiny islets located just off the coast of Coron, renowned for its vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life. Translating to "Seven Sins," this picturesque spot offers crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and diving. Visitors can explore the rich underwater ecosystem, teeming with colorful fish, sea turtles, and magnificent corals. The stunning scenery, marked by rocky formations and lush vegetation, makes it a favorite among nature enthusiasts and photographers alike. Siete Pecados is not only a serene escape but also a testament to the rich biodiversity that thrives in Coron’s waters.
