FUN FACTS ABOUT CORON

1. CORON IS PART OF THE CALAMIAN ISLANDS

Coron is a striking island located in the northern part of Palawan, Philippines, and is a prominent member of the Calamian Group of Islands. This archipelago features a stunning array of limestone cliffs, crystal-clear lagoons, and diverse marine life. Known for its breathtaking scenery and rich biodiversity, Coron is a popular destination for diving, snorkeling, and exploring vibrant coral reefs. Its unique geography and cultural heritage make it an invaluable part of the Calamian islands, attracting adventurers and nature lovers from around the world. The main tourist town (Coron Town Proper) is on Busuanga Island, while the iconic tourist spots (Kayangan Lake, Twin Lagoon) are on the protected Coron Island.

2. WW2 JAPANESE SHIPWRECKS

Coron is renowned for its hauntingly beautiful World War II Japanese shipwrecks, which lie submerged in its crystal-clear waters. These wrecks, remnants of the Battle of Coron Bay in 1944, have become popular diving spots, attracting enthusiasts and history buffs alike. The sites offer a unique glimpse into history while showcasing vibrant coral reefs that have enveloped the sunken vessels over the decades. Each shipwreck tells a story of valor and conflict, making them both an archaeological treasure and a dive into the rich underwater heritage of Coron.

3. INDIGENOUS PEOPLES OF CORON

The indigenous populations of Coron, primarily the Tagbanua and Calamianes tribes, are the traditional stewards of the land and waters in this stunning region. Their rich cultural heritage is marked by unique practices, beliefs, and a profound connection to nature. The Tagbanua people, known for their traditional fishing and farming techniques, have a sophisticated understanding of the environment that informs their sustainable practices. With a strong emphasis on community and ancestral knowledge, these indigenous groups play a crucial role in preserving the ecological balance of their islands, making them essential guardians of both cultural identity and environmental stewardship in Coron.
